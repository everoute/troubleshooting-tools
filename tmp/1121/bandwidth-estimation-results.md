```
Estimated_BW = cwnd × MSS × 8 / RTT
             = cwnd × 1448 × 8 / (RTT_ms / 1000)
             = cwnd × 11584 / RTT_ms  (bps)
```

### Key Findings

1. **Most samples are NIC-limited**: ~80% of samples have theoretical BW > 25 Gbps
2. **RTT spikes cause low BW estimates**: When RTT > 5ms, BW drops significantly
3. **Bind has higher cwnd**: avg 8865 vs 3346 for Nobind
4. **Nobind has lower RTT**: avg 1.29ms vs 2.13ms for Bind
5. **Both achieve similar average estimated BW**: ~22.7 Gbps
6. **Actual delivery_rate (~15 Gbps) is ~34% lower than estimates**

### RTT Impact Analysis

The non-linear relationship (BW = 1/RTT) means:
- Low RTT (< 1ms): BW capped at NIC limit (25 Gbps)
- High RTT (> 5ms): BW drops to 5-15 Gbps range
- A few high-RTT samples disproportionately impact average throughput
